What are those areas?

Cloning
Among the various areas of biotechnology, I am particularly interested in cloning. Cloning is the process of creating an exact copy of a living organism or its part using its genetic material. It can be beneficial for research, medicine, or species preservation. For example, cloning can help grow identical plants or create animals with specific characteristics.

Organ Cultivation
Another area that fascinates me is organ cultivation. Organ cultivation involves creating living organs in laboratory conditions for transplantation into the human body. This can assist patients who require organ transplants but do not have access to donor organs. For instance, growing skin, hearts, or kidneys can help save lives and improve people's quality of life.

Prosthetics and Neural Interactions
Prosthetics, specifically prostheses that interact with the nervous system, currently interest me the most. Prostheses that interact with the nervous system transmit signals between the brain and the replacement device, enabling individuals to control the prosthesis through their thoughts or sense touches and movements through the nervous system, bringing prosthetics closer to natural body interaction.

The Interplay of Computer Science and Biology
It should be noted that without computer science this field would not exist at all. At first glance, biotechnology may seem solely related to biology, but in most cases, only the outcomes are showcased, while computer science plays a significant role in all the research, design, and programming aspects of biotechnology. It may seem that these two sciences do not blend but biotechnology has proven otherwise.
